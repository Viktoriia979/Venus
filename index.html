Venus:

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's "sister planet" because it is similar in size, mass, and structure. It is the closest planet to Earth and one of the brightest objects in the night sky. Venus is covered by thick clouds made of sulfuric acid, which hide its surface from view. Its atmosphere is mostly carbon dioxide, creating a strong greenhouse effect that makes Venus the hottest planet in the Solar System, with temperatures reaching about 465°C (869°F).

Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets, so the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east. One day on Venus lasts about 243 Earth days, while one year on Venus is only about 225 Earth days. The planet has no moons or rings. Scientists have used spacecraft and radar technology to study its surface, which contains mountains, volcanoes, and large plains formed by ancient lava flows.

Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. Because it shines so brightly, it is often called the "Morning Star" or "Evening Star," even though it is a planet and not a star.
